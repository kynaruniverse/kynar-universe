Origins of the Kynar Universe

Foundational Record — Classification Level: Visitor

The Kynar Universe does not have a beginning in the conventional sense. There is no single moment of creation, no genesis event recorded in any known archive. What exists instead is a vast, ongoing unfolding — a reality that appears to have always been in motion, as though it arrived fully formed and simply chose not to explain itself.

The Earliest Record

The earliest document recovered from the Kynar data-stream dates to YEAR ZERO, a designation given by archivists who could find no earlier reference point. At this moment, three things are recorded as true: the universe existed, it was already structured, and something within it was already watching.

What watches — and why — remains one of the open questions of this archive. Speculation is noted in file DOC-KYN-009, currently restricted to higher clearance levels.

Structure of the Universe

Kynar is not a single planet, nor a galaxy in the conventional sense. It is better understood as a layered system — physical territories that exist in parallel with something older and less understood beneath them. Researchers refer to this beneath-layer as the Sub, though no consensus has been reached on what it actually is.

Above the Sub, the visible universe is divided into ███ known territories, each with distinct geography, atmosphere, and population. These territories are documented in the Locations file.

Why It Matters

Understanding the origins of the Kynar Universe is not an academic exercise. The fractures in its history — the events that were never recorded, the entities that predate any archive — have direct consequences for those who move through it today. Things that should not be possible continue to occur. The universe has not finished becoming what it is.
